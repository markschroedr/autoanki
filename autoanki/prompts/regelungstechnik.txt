Regelungstechnik specialization

- Treat the supplied material as control-engineering study material and preserve technically important distinctions.
- When the source is German, write the cards in clear German.
- Be especially careful that a question does not reveal its answer through verbs such as "einschränken", "erstellen", "darstellen", or "ermöglichen".
- Distinguish concepts such as the general transfer function \(G(s)\), the frequency response \(G(j\omega)\), poles and zeros, stability definitions, and steady-state sinusoidal behavior precisely when they occur in the source.
- For formulas, test the meaning, applicability, or relationship expressed by the formula instead of merely asking for symbol transcription.
- For cloze cards containing math, keep the cloze wrapper outside the MathJax delimiters.
- Use the following domain buckets as guidance when organizing the material:
  - grundlagen: linearity, time invariance, causality, relative degree, direct feedthrough, stability definitions, step response, and impulse response.
  - laplace: Laplace transform, transfer functions, poles and zeros, partial fractions, stability criteria in the s-plane.
  - wirkungsplan: block diagrams, simplification rules, feedback loops, and deriving transfer functions from diagrams.
  - bode: Bode plots, frequency response, magnitude and phase, asymptotes, and frequency-domain controller design.
  - ortskurve: Nyquist plots and criteria, phase margin, and gain margin.
  - zustandsraum: state-space models, system matrices, eigenvalues, controllability, observability, observers, and state feedback.
  - nichtlinear: operating-point linearization, phase portraits, Lyapunov stability, saturation, and limit cycles.
  - zeitdiskret: sampling, Z-transform, discrete transfer functions and controllers, and stability in the z-plane.
- If these domain bucket names are present in the configured allowed tags, prefer the matching bucket. Otherwise use the closest configured generic tag; never invent an unavailable tag.
